### 1. Purpose
The Disclosing Party holds certain confidential and proprietary information regarding the design, schematic, and layout of a custom ESP32-S3 hardware controller board (the "Project"). The Receiving Party is being hired or consulted to review, route, or manufacture this PCB. This Agreement is intended to prevent the unauthorized disclosure of Confidential Information.

### 2. Definition of Confidential Information
"Confidential Information" shall include all data, materials, products, technology, computer programs, specifications, manuals, software, firmware, and other information disclosed or submitted, orally, in writing, or by any other media, to the Receiving Party by the Disclosing Party. 
Specific to this project, this includes but is not limited to:
* The KiCad Schematic files (`.kicad_sch`)
* The KiCad PCB Layout files (`.kicad_pcb`)
* Gerber files, BOM (Bill of Materials), and Pick-and-Place files.
* Firmware concepts, C++ source code, and exact component combinations.

### 3. Obligations of the Receiving Party
The Receiving Party agrees that they shall:
a) Hold and maintain the Confidential Information in strict confidence.
b) Not share, distribute, sell, publish, or publicly display (including on social media or portfolios) any screenshots, files, or details of the PCB without express written consent from the Disclosing Party.
c) Not use the Confidential Information to reverse-engineer, mass-produce, or sell competing hardware.
d) Restrict access to the Confidential Information exclusively to employees or subcontractors who require the information to perform the requested PCB services, ensuring those parties are also bound by similar confidentiality rules.

### 4. Return or Destruction of Materials
Upon the completion of the PCB services, or at the written request of the Disclosing Party, the Receiving Party shall immediately destroy or return all digital and physical copies of the Confidential Information (including all KiCad and Gerber files) from their computers, servers, and storage devices.

### 5. Term
The non-disclosure provisions of this Agreement shall survive the termination of this Agreement and the Receiving Party's duty to hold Confidential Information in confidence shall remain in effect for a period of **Three (3) years** from the date of disclosure.
